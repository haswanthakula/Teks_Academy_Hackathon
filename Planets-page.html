Mercury


Size: Smallest planet in the Solar System.
Distance from the Sun: Closest.
Surface: Rocky, heavily cratered.
Atmosphere: Thin, mostly oxygen, sodium, hydrogen.
Temperature: Extreme fluctuations; very hot during the day (up to 430°C) and very cold at night (down to -180°C).

FunFact : Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system, and the nearest to the Sun.

Venus


Size: Similar in size to Earth.
Distance from the Sun: 2nd planet.
Surface: Volcanic, mountainous, covered in thick clouds of sulfuric acid.
Atmosphere: Thick, mostly carbon dioxide; causes extreme greenhouse effect.
Temperature: Hottest planet (up to 465°C).
Interesting Fact: Venus spins backward (retrograde rotation).

FunFact : Venus is the second planet from the Sun, and Earth's closest planetary neighbor.

Earth


Size: Largest of the inner planets.
Distance from the Sun: 3rd planet.
Surface: 70% covered by water, varied terrain (mountains, plains, oceans).
Atmosphere: Nitrogen, oxygen-rich, supports life.
Temperature: Moderate and suitable for life.
Interesting Fact: Only known planet to support life.

FunFact : Earth – our home planet – is the third planet from the Sun, and the fifth largest planet.

Mars


Size: About half the size of Earth.
Distance from the Sun: 4th planet.
Surface: Dusty, rocky, with the largest volcano (Olympus Mons) and canyon (Valles Marineris).
Atmosphere: Thin, mostly carbon dioxide.
Temperature: Cold (-60°C on average).
Interesting Fact: Often called the "Red Planet" due to its iron oxide (rust) surface.

FunFact : Mars – the fourth planet from the Sun – is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere.

Jupiter


Size: Largest planet in the Solar System.
Distance from the Sun: 5th planet.
Surface: Gas giant, mainly hydrogen and helium.
Atmosphere: Stormy, home to the Great Red Spot, a massive storm.
Temperature: Cold (-145°C).
Interesting Fact: Jupiter has at least 79 moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System.

FunFact : Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system – if it were a hollow shell, 1,000 Earths could fit inside.

Saturn


Size: 2nd largest planet.
Distance from the Sun: 6th planet.
Surface: Gas giant, primarily hydrogen and helium.
Atmosphere: Bands of storms, with winds reaching up to 1,800 km/h.
Temperature: Cold (-178°C).
Interesting Fact: Famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock particles.

FunFact : Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun, the second-largest planet in our solar system.

Uranus


Size: 3rd largest planet.
Distance from the Sun: 7th planet.
Surface: Ice giant, with water, ammonia, and methane beneath a hydrogen-helium atmosphere.
Atmosphere: Methane gives it a blue-green color.
Temperature: Extremely cold (-224°C).
Interesting Fact: Uranus rotates on its side, making its poles face the Sun.

FunFact : The seventh planet from the Sun, Uranus has the third largest diameter of planets in our solar system.

Neptune


Size: 4th largest planet.
Distance from the Sun: 8th and farthest known planet.
Surface: Ice giant, similar to Uranus.
Atmosphere: Violent storms, with the fastest winds in the Solar System (up to 2,100 km/h).
Temperature: Very cold (-214°C).
Interesting Fact: Neptune's moon Triton has geysers that spew nitrogen gas.

FunFact : Neptune is the eighth and most distant planet in our solar system.
